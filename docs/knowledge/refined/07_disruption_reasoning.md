# Disruption Reasoning

Disruptions are when reality breaks your assumptions.

The system must detect and react, not just operate under normal conditions.

---

# Core Question

What happens when things don’t go as expected?

---

# What a Disruption Is

A disruption is a deviation from plan.

Examples:

- demand spike or drop
- supplier delay
- transportation failure
- inventory mismatch

Normal logic assumes stability.
Disruptions violate that.

---

# Why Disruptions Matter

Most systems work fine in normal conditions.

They fail during stress.

Real value comes from:

- detecting issues early
- adapting decisions quickly

---

# Types of Disruptions

## Demand-Side

- sudden increase (promotion, panic buying)
- sudden drop (seasonality shift, demand collapse)

---

## Supply-Side

- delayed shipments
- supplier shortages
- logistics breakdown

---

## Internal State

- inventory inaccuracies
- system delays
- data issues

---

# Detection vs Reaction

Two separate problems:

## Detection

Identify that something is wrong.

- demand deviates from forecast
- lead time increases
- inventory drops unexpectedly

---

## Reaction

Adjust decisions.

- reorder earlier
- increase safety stock
- reallocate inventory

---

# Signal vs Noise

Not every deviation is a disruption.

Key challenge:

Is this real change or random fluctuation?

Overreacting:
- creates instability

Underreacting:
- creates risk

---

# Role of Thresholds

Most systems use thresholds.

Examples:

- demand > forecast by X%
- lead time > expected by Y days

Thresholds trigger attention.

---

# System Behavior Under Disruption

Normal mode:
- stable policies
- predictable flow

Disruption mode:
- more aggressive decisions
- faster response
- higher safety margins

---

# Tradeoffs

## Fast Reaction

- reduces risk
- can overcorrect

---

## Slow Reaction

- stable system
- may miss critical events

---

## Conservative Policy

- more inventory
- higher cost

---

## Aggressive Policy

- lean inventory
- higher risk

---

# Where It Fits

Disruption logic sits across the system.

- uses forecast vs actual
- uses inventory signals
- affects replenishment decisions

It is not a separate module.
It is a layer of awareness.

---

# Common Mistakes

- treating all deviations equally  
- reacting to noise  
- ignoring early warning signals  
- hardcoding responses without flexibility  

---

# Mental Model

Disruptions are stress tests in real time.

They show where your system is weak.

---

# One-Line Summary

Disruption reasoning detects when real-world conditions deviate from assumptions and adjusts system behavior to maintain stability under uncertainty.
